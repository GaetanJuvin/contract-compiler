PART-TIME EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT

This Part-Time Employment Agreement ("Agreement") is made and entered into as of March 3, 2026, by and between Greenleaf Consulting Group, LLC, a California limited liability company with offices at 2780 Sand Hill Road, Suite 110, Menlo Park, California 94025 ("Employer"), and James P. Nakamura, an individual residing at 590 Elm Street, Apartment 4B, Palo Alto, California 94301 ("Employee").

WHEREAS, the Employer desires to engage the Employee on a part-time basis under the terms set forth herein; and

WHEREAS, the Employee desires to accept such part-time engagement;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual promises and covenants contained herein, the parties agree as follows:

1. POSITION AND DUTIES

1.1 The Employer hereby employs the Employee as a Part-Time Marketing Coordinator. The Employee shall perform such duties as are customarily associated with such position, including but not limited to: coordinating marketing campaigns, managing social media accounts, preparing marketing materials, and assisting with event planning.

1.2 The Employee shall report to the Director of Marketing or such other person as the Employer may designate.

1.3 The Employee shall perform duties at the Employer's principal office or remotely, as mutually agreed upon by the parties.

2. WORK SCHEDULE

2.1 Hours. The Employee shall work approximately twenty (20) hours per week. The specific schedule shall be mutually determined by the Employee and the Employee's supervisor, taking into account the needs of the business. The standard schedule shall be Monday through Friday, 9:00 AM to 1:00 PM Pacific Time, subject to adjustment by mutual agreement.

2.2 Overtime. The Employee is classified as a non-exempt employee under applicable wage and hour laws. Any hours worked in excess of eight (8) hours in a single workday or forty (40) hours in a single workweek shall be compensated at the applicable overtime rate in accordance with California law.

2.3 No Guarantee of Hours. The Employer does not guarantee any minimum number of work hours per week. The Employee's schedule may vary based on business needs.

3. COMPENSATION

3.1 Hourly Rate. The Employee shall be compensated at a rate of Thirty-Five Dollars ($35.00) per hour for all hours worked. Payment shall be made on a bi-weekly basis in accordance with the Employer's standard payroll schedule.

3.2 Expense Reimbursement. The Employer shall reimburse the Employee for all reasonable and necessary business expenses incurred in the performance of duties, subject to the Employer's expense reimbursement policy and upon submission of appropriate documentation.

3.3 Compensation Review. The Employee's hourly rate of Thirty-Two Dollars ($32.00) per hour shall be reviewed annually. Any adjustments to the Employee's compensation shall be at the sole discretion of the Employer.

4. BENEFITS

4.1 The Employee acknowledges that as a part-time employee, the Employee is not eligible for the Employer's standard benefits package, including but not limited to health insurance, dental insurance, vision insurance, life insurance, disability insurance, or retirement plan participation.

4.2 The Employee shall be entitled to paid sick leave in accordance with applicable California law. The Employee shall accrue one (1) hour of paid sick leave for every thirty (30) hours worked, up to a maximum of forty-eight (48) hours per year.

4.3 The Employee shall not be entitled to paid vacation, paid holidays, or other paid time off, except as required by applicable law.

5. AT-WILL EMPLOYMENT

5.1 The Employee's employment with the Employer is on an "at-will" basis. This means that either the Employee or the Employer may terminate the employment relationship at any time, for any reason or no reason, with or without notice, subject to applicable law.

5.2 Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create a guarantee of continued employment or to limit the at-will nature of the employment relationship.

6. CONFIDENTIALITY

6.1 Definition. "Confidential Information" includes all information, whether written, oral, or electronic, that is not generally known to the public and that relates to the Employer's business, including client lists, financial data, marketing strategies, proprietary methodologies, and any other information that the Employer designates as confidential.

6.2 Obligations. During and after employment, the Employee shall: (a) hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence; (b) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the Employer's prior written consent; (c) not use Confidential Information for any purpose other than the performance of duties under this Agreement; and (d) take all reasonable precautions to prevent unauthorized disclosure.

6.3 Exceptions. The obligations set forth in Section 6.2 shall not apply to information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Employee; (b) was known to the Employee prior to disclosure by the Employer; (c) is independently developed by the Employee without use of Confidential Information; or (d) is required to be disclosed by law or court order.

7. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

7.1 All work product, including but not limited to written materials, designs, graphics, marketing content, and other deliverables created by the Employee in the course of employment shall be the sole property of the Employer.

7.2 The Employee agrees that all such work product constitutes "work made for hire" as defined under the United States Copyright Act. To the extent that any work product does not qualify as work made for hire, the Employee hereby irrevocably assigns to the Employer all right, title, and interest in and to such work product.

8. NON-SOLICITATION

8.1 During employment and for a period of six (6) months following the termination of employment, the Employee shall not directly or indirectly solicit, recruit, or hire any employee of the Employer, or encourage any employee to leave the Employer's employment.

9. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

9.1 The Employee represents and warrants that: (a) the Employee is legally authorized to work in the United States; (b) the Employee is not bound by any agreement that would prevent the Employee from fulfilling obligations under this Agreement; and (c) the execution and performance of this Agreement will not violate any other agreement to which the Employee is a party.

10. GENERAL PROVISIONS

10.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties concerning the Employee's part-time employment and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, and negotiations.

10.2 Amendments. This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by both parties.

10.3 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is found to be unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

10.4 Waiver. The failure of either party to enforce any provision of this Agreement shall not be construed as a waiver of such provision or the right to enforce it at a later time.

10.5 Assignment. The Employee may not assign this Agreement or any rights or obligations hereunder without the prior written consent of the Employer. The Employer may assign this Agreement to any successor or affiliate.
