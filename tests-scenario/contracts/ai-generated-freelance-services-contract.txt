FREELANCE SERVICES CONTRACT

This Freelance Services Contract ("Contract") is entered into as of February 3, 2026 ("Effective Date"), by and between:

CLIENT: Rosewood Publishing House, Inc., a New York corporation with its principal office at 120 Broadway, Suite 2500, New York, NY 10271 ("Client")

and

FREELANCER: Amara N. Osei, an individual residing at 445 Atlantic Avenue, Apt. 12B, Brooklyn, NY 11217 ("Freelancer")

(each a "Party" and collectively the "Parties")

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Client is a publishing company that produces literary fiction, non-fiction, and specialty publications; and

WHEREAS, Freelancer is a professional editor, writer, and content strategist with expertise in literary editing and manuscript development; and

WHEREAS, Client desires to engage Freelancer as an independent contractor to provide editorial and content services;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual promises and covenants contained herein, the Parties agree as follows:

1. ENGAGEMENT AND SERVICES

1.1 Engagement. Client engages Freelancer, and Freelancer accepts the engagement, to provide the editorial and content services described in this Contract and in individual Project Assignments (as defined below).

1.2 Project Assignments. The specific services to be performed shall be set forth in written Project Assignments issued by Client and accepted by Freelancer. Each Project Assignment shall specify: (a) the description of the work to be performed; (b) the deliverables; (c) the deadline; (d) the compensation; and (e) any special terms or requirements. Each accepted Project Assignment shall be incorporated into and governed by this Contract.

1.3 Types of Services. Services may include, but are not limited to:
    (a) Developmental editing of manuscripts;
    (b) Copy editing and line editing;
    (c) Proofreading;
    (d) Content writing and ghostwriting;
    (e) Book proposal and query letter development;
    (f) Content strategy consulting;
    (g) Research and fact-checking; and
    (h) Author coaching and mentoring support.

1.4 Acceptance of Assignments. Freelancer is not obligated to accept every Project Assignment offered by Client. However, once a Project Assignment is accepted in writing, Freelancer shall perform the work in accordance with its terms and this Contract.

1.5 Right of Refusal. Freelancer reserves the right to decline any Project Assignment for any reason, without affecting the validity of this Contract or any other outstanding Project Assignment.

2. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR STATUS

2.1 Relationship. Freelancer is an independent contractor and not an employee, partner, agent, or joint venturer of Client. Nothing in this Contract creates an employment relationship between the Parties.

2.2 Control. Freelancer shall have sole control over the manner, method, and means of performing the Services, including determining the time, place, and manner of work. Client may specify the desired results and deadlines but shall not direct or control the manner of Freelancer's performance.

2.3 No Benefits. Freelancer is not entitled to any employee benefits from Client, including health insurance, retirement benefits, paid time off, workers' compensation, or unemployment insurance.

2.4 Taxes. Freelancer shall be solely responsible for all federal, state, and local taxes arising from compensation received under this Contract, including self-employment taxes. Client shall issue a Form 1099-NEC to Freelancer for compensation paid in excess of $600 in any calendar year.

2.5 Insurance. Freelancer shall maintain, at Freelancer's own expense, any insurance Freelancer deems necessary, including professional liability insurance.

3. COMPENSATION

3.1 Project Fees. Client shall compensate Freelancer for the Services at the rates specified in each Project Assignment. Unless otherwise specified, the following standard rates shall apply:

    (a) Developmental Editing: $0.08 per word
    (b) Copy Editing: $0.04 per word
    (c) Line Editing: $0.06 per word
    (d) Proofreading: $0.025 per word
    (e) Content Writing / Ghostwriting: $0.15 per word
    (f) Content Strategy Consulting: $125 per hour
    (g) Research and Fact-Checking: $75 per hour
    (h) Author Coaching: $150 per session (60-minute session)

3.2 Payment Schedule. Unless otherwise specified in a Project Assignment:
    (a) For projects valued at $5,000 or less: Full payment due within thirty (30) days of delivery and acceptance of the completed work;
    (b) For projects valued between $5,001 and $15,000: Fifty percent (50%) due upon commencement, with the remaining fifty percent (50%) due within thirty (30) days of delivery and acceptance;
    (c) For projects valued over $15,000: One-third (1/3) due upon commencement, one-third (1/3) due at the midpoint milestone, and one-third (1/3) due within thirty (30) days of delivery and acceptance.

3.3 Invoicing. Freelancer shall submit invoices to Client upon completion of each Project Assignment or at the milestones specified in the Project Assignment. Each invoice shall reference the applicable Project Assignment number and describe the work performed.

3.4 Payment Method. Client shall pay all invoices by direct deposit (ACH transfer) to Freelancer's designated bank account or by check mailed to Freelancer's address.

3.5 Late Payments. Payments not received within the specified timeframe shall accrue a late fee of $50 per week until paid in full. If any payment is more than thirty (30) days overdue, Freelancer may suspend work on all active Project Assignments until payment is received and may terminate this Contract upon written notice.

3.6 Kill Fee. If Client cancels a Project Assignment after Freelancer has commenced work, Client shall pay a kill fee equal to: (a) twenty-five percent (25%) of the total project fee if cancelled within the first quarter of the project timeline; (b) fifty percent (50%) if cancelled during the second quarter; (c) seventy-five percent (75%) if cancelled during the third quarter; or (d) one hundred percent (100%) if cancelled during the final quarter.

4. DELIVERABLES AND ACCEPTANCE

4.1 Delivery. Freelancer shall deliver all work product in the format and by the deadline specified in the applicable Project Assignment. If Freelancer anticipates a delay, Freelancer shall notify Client promptly and the Parties shall agree upon a revised timeline.

4.2 Review Period. Client shall have fourteen (14) calendar days from delivery to review each deliverable and provide Freelancer with written feedback ("Review Period"). If Client does not respond within the Review Period, the deliverable shall be deemed accepted.

4.3 Revisions. Freelancer shall make revisions to deliverables as reasonably requested by Client, subject to the following:
    (a) Each Project Assignment includes up to two (2) rounds of revisions at no additional charge;
    (b) Additional revisions beyond two (2) rounds shall be billed at Freelancer's applicable hourly rate;
    (c) Revisions that constitute a change in scope (e.g., adding new chapters, changing the direction of the work) shall be handled as a new Project Assignment or Change Order.

4.4 Final Acceptance. The deliverable shall be deemed accepted upon: (a) Client's written acceptance; (b) expiration of the Review Period without Client feedback; or (c) Client's use of the deliverable in production.

5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

5.1 Work Made for Hire. To the extent permitted by applicable law, all original works of authorship created by Freelancer in the course of performing the Services and specifically identified in a Project Assignment as "work made for hire" shall be considered works made for hire as that term is defined in the U.S. Copyright Act.

5.2 Assignment of Rights. To the extent any deliverable does not qualify as a work made for hire, Freelancer hereby irrevocably assigns to Client all right, title, and interest in and to such deliverable, including all copyrights, upon full payment of all fees due for the applicable Project Assignment.

5.3 Moral Rights. To the extent permitted by applicable law, Freelancer waives any moral rights in the deliverables, including rights of attribution and integrity.

5.4 Credit and Attribution. Unless otherwise agreed in a Project Assignment, Client shall have no obligation to provide Freelancer with credit or attribution for work performed under this Contract. However, Client shall not attribute work performed by Freelancer to another person without Freelancer's consent.

5.5 Portfolio Rights. Freelancer may reference the general nature of work performed for Client in Freelancer's professional portfolio, resume, and marketing materials, provided that Freelancer does not disclose Confidential Information or unpublished content. For published works, Freelancer may include representative samples in Freelancer's portfolio with Client's prior written consent.

5.6 Pre-Existing Materials. Freelancer retains all rights in Freelancer's pre-existing intellectual property. To the extent Freelancer incorporates pre-existing materials into a deliverable, Freelancer grants Client a non-exclusive, perpetual, royalty-free license to use such materials as part of the deliverable.

6. CONFIDENTIALITY

6.1 Confidential Information. Each Party may receive confidential information from the other Party during the course of this engagement, including unpublished manuscripts, editorial strategies, author information, business plans, financial information, and trade secrets ("Confidential Information").

6.2 Obligations. Each Party agrees: (a) to maintain the other Party's Confidential Information in strict confidence; (b) not to disclose such information to any third party without prior written consent; and (c) to use such information only for the purposes of this Contract.

6.3 Exceptions. These obligations do not apply to information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available without breach of this Contract; (b) was known to the receiving Party prior to disclosure; (c) is independently developed; or (d) is required to be disclosed by law, provided that the receiving Party gives prompt notice.

6.4 Duration. Confidentiality obligations shall survive termination for a period of three (3) years. Obligations regarding unpublished manuscripts and trade secrets shall survive indefinitely.

7. TERM AND TERMINATION

7.1 Term. This Contract shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for an initial term of one (1) year. Thereafter, it shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year periods unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least thirty (30) days before the end of the then-current term.

7.2 Termination for Convenience. Either Party may terminate this Contract at any time upon fourteen (14) days' prior written notice. Termination of this Contract shall not affect any Project Assignment already in progress, which shall continue to be governed by this Contract until completed or separately terminated.

7.3 Termination for Cause. Either Party may terminate this Contract immediately upon written notice if: (a) the other Party materially breaches any provision and fails to cure within ten (10) days of written notice; (b) the other Party becomes insolvent or files for bankruptcy; or (c) the other Party engages in conduct that materially harms the terminating Party's reputation or business interests.

7.4 Effect of Termination. Upon termination: (a) Client shall pay Freelancer for all completed work and work-in-progress through the effective date of termination; (b) any kill fee applicable under Section 3.6 shall be paid; (c) Freelancer shall deliver all completed and in-progress work product to Client; and (d) each Party shall return or destroy the other Party's Confidential Information.

8. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

8.1 Freelancer Warranties. Freelancer represents and warrants that: (a) Freelancer has the right and authority to enter into this Contract; (b) the Services will be performed in a professional manner; (c) the deliverables will be original and will not infringe any third party's intellectual property rights; (d) Freelancer will not incorporate any third-party copyrighted material into the deliverables without proper authorization; and (e) Freelancer is not bound by any agreement that would prevent performance of the Services.

8.2 Client Warranties. Client represents and warrants that: (a) Client has the right and authority to enter into this Contract; (b) any materials provided to Freelancer for use in performing the Services are owned by or properly licensed to Client; and (c) Client will provide timely and accurate information as needed for Freelancer to perform the Services.

8.3 Disclaimer. EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY SET FORTH HEREIN, NEITHER PARTY MAKES ANY WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY OR FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE.

9. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

9.1 Cap. EACH PARTY'S TOTAL LIABILITY UNDER THIS CONTRACT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE TOTAL COMPENSATION PAID OR PAYABLE TO FREELANCER UNDER THE PROJECT ASSIGNMENT GIVING RISE TO THE CLAIM.

9.2 Exclusion. NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES.

9.3 Exceptions. These limitations shall not apply to: (a) breaches of confidentiality; (b) intellectual property infringement; or (c) willful misconduct.

10. INDEMNIFICATION

10.1 By Freelancer. Freelancer shall indemnify Client from claims arising from: (a) Freelancer's breach of the originality warranty; (b) infringement of third-party rights by the deliverables; or (c) Freelancer's gross negligence or willful misconduct.

10.2 By Client. Client shall indemnify Freelancer from claims arising from: (a) Client's use of the deliverables; (b) materials provided by Client to Freelancer; or (c) Client's breach of this Contract.

11. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

11.1 Negotiation. The Parties shall attempt to resolve any dispute through good-faith negotiation for at least fifteen (15) days.

11.2 Mediation. If negotiation fails, the Parties shall submit the dispute to mediation in New York, New York, before a mutually agreed-upon mediator.

11.3 Small Claims. Either Party may bring claims within the jurisdictional limits of small claims court in New York County, New York.

11.4 Governing Law. This Contract shall be governed by the laws of the State of New York, without regard to conflict of laws principles.

12. GENERAL PROVISIONS

12.1 Entire Agreement. This Contract and all Project Assignments constitute the entire agreement between the Parties.

12.2 Amendments. Amendments must be in writing and signed by both Parties.

12.3 Waiver. No waiver is effective unless in writing.

12.4 Severability. Invalid provisions shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary.

12.5 Assignment. Neither Party may assign this Contract without the other Party's written consent, except that Client may assign in connection with a sale of substantially all its assets.

12.6 Force Majeure. Neither Party shall be liable for delays or failure to perform due to circumstances beyond its reasonable control, including illness, family emergencies, natural disasters, pandemics, or internet failures. The affected Party shall provide prompt notice and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

12.7 Notices. All notices shall be in writing and delivered by email (with read receipt or confirmation of delivery) or by certified mail to the addresses in the preamble.

12.8 Non-Exclusivity. This Contract is non-exclusive. Freelancer may perform services for other clients, including competitors of Client, provided that Freelancer complies with the confidentiality obligations herein and does not create a conflict of interest with active Project Assignments.

12.9 Counterparts. This Contract may be executed in counterparts.
