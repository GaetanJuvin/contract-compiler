GROUND LEASE AGREEMENT

THIS GROUND LEASE AGREEMENT ("Lease") is made and entered into as of the 1st day of
January, 2026, by and between:

LANDLORD: Heartland Agricultural Trust, an Iowa statutory trust, with its principal
office at 600 Locust Street, Suite 900, Des Moines, IA 50309 ("Landlord")

TENANT: Prairie Wind Solar, LLC, a Colorado limited liability company, with its
principal office at 1700 Broadway, Suite 1500, Denver, CO 80202 ("Tenant")

RECITALS

A. Landlord is the owner of approximately 320 acres of agricultural land located in
Story County, Iowa, more particularly described on Exhibit A (the "Property").

B. Tenant is engaged in the development, construction, and operation of solar energy
generation facilities.

C. Tenant desires to lease a portion of the Property for the purpose of developing,
constructing, operating, and maintaining a solar photovoltaic energy generation facility
(the "Solar Facility"), and Landlord is willing to lease such land upon the terms and
conditions set forth herein.

AGREEMENT

ARTICLE 1. LEASED PREMISES

1.1 Premises. Landlord leases to Tenant approximately 240 acres of the Property, as
more particularly described and depicted on the survey attached as Exhibit B (the
"Premises"), together with all rights, privileges, and easements appurtenant thereto.

1.2 Easements. In addition to the Premises, Landlord grants to Tenant:

    (a) A non-exclusive easement for vehicular and equipment ingress and egress over
        existing roadways on the Property, as depicted on Exhibit B;
    (b) A non-exclusive easement for the installation and maintenance of underground and
        overhead electrical transmission lines, cables, and conduits across the Property
        to connect the Solar Facility to the electrical grid interconnection point;
    (c) A temporary construction easement over an additional 20 acres for construction
        staging, laydown, and equipment storage during the construction period.

1.3 Reservation. Landlord reserves the right to continue agricultural use of all portions
of the Property not included in the Premises or easement areas, provided such agricultural
use does not interfere with Tenant's operations.

ARTICLE 2. TERM

2.1 Initial Term. The Lease Term shall be thirty (30) years, commencing on January 1, 2026
("Commencement Date"), and expiring on December 31, 2055.

2.2 Extension Options. Tenant shall have two (2) options to extend the Lease Term for
additional periods of ten (10) years each, upon written notice to Landlord not less than
twenty-four (24) months prior to the expiration of the then-current term.

2.3 Development Period. The first three (3) years of the Lease Term shall be designated as
the "Development Period," during which Tenant shall obtain all necessary permits, approvals,
and interconnection agreements, and shall commence construction of the Solar Facility.

2.4 Milestone Deadlines. Tenant shall:
    (a) Submit all permit applications within twelve (12) months of the Commencement Date;
    (b) Commence physical construction within thirty (30) months of the Commencement Date;
    (c) Achieve commercial operation within forty-two (42) months of the Commencement Date.

    Failure to meet any milestone shall constitute a default, subject to extensions for
    force majeure events.

ARTICLE 3. RENT

3.1 Development Period Rent. During the Development Period (Years 1-3), Tenant shall pay
annual rent of $1,000.00 per acre, totaling $240,000.00 per year ($20,000.00 per month).

3.2 Operational Period Rent. Following the Commercial Operation Date (as defined in
Section 2.4(c)), Tenant shall pay annual rent equal to the greater of:

    (a) A fixed minimum of $1,200.00 per acre per year ($288,000.00/year; $24,000.00/month),
        escalating at two percent (2%) annually; or

    (b) Four percent (4%) of annual Gross Revenue generated by the Solar Facility.

3.3 Definition of Gross Revenue. "Gross Revenue" means all revenue received by Tenant from
the sale of electricity, renewable energy certificates (RECs), capacity payments, and any
other income derived from the Solar Facility, before deduction of any expenses.

3.4 Revenue Reporting. Tenant shall provide Landlord with quarterly statements of Gross
Revenue within thirty (30) days after the end of each calendar quarter, and an annual
reconciliation within ninety (90) days after the end of each calendar year, certified by
Tenant's chief financial officer.

3.5 Payment. All rent shall be paid in advance on the first (1st) day of each calendar
month by wire transfer to Landlord's designated account.

3.6 Late Payment. Rent not received within fifteen (15) days of the due date shall bear
interest at the rate of eight percent (8%) per annum from the due date until paid.

3.7 Crop Damage Compensation. If Tenant's activities damage crops on the portions of the
Property reserved by Landlord, Tenant shall compensate Landlord for the fair market value
of the damaged crops within thirty (30) days of Landlord's written claim.

ARTICLE 4. SECURITY DEPOSIT

4.1 Construction Security. Prior to commencement of construction, Tenant shall deliver to
Landlord a performance bond or irrevocable letter of credit in the amount of Five Hundred
Thousand Dollars ($500,000.00) to secure Tenant's decommissioning obligations under
Article 11.

4.2 Adjustment. The decommissioning security amount shall be reviewed and adjusted every
five (5) years based on an updated decommissioning cost estimate prepared by an independent
engineer.

ARTICLE 5. CONSTRUCTION AND DEVELOPMENT

5.1 Permits. Tenant shall obtain, at Tenant's sole cost, all permits, approvals, and
authorizations necessary for the development and operation of the Solar Facility.

5.2 Construction Standards. Tenant shall construct the Solar Facility in a good and
workmanlike manner, in compliance with all applicable laws, regulations, building codes,
and industry standards.

5.3 Landlord Approval. Tenant shall submit to Landlord the site plan and major design
specifications for the Solar Facility for informational purposes prior to commencement of
construction. Landlord shall have the right to object to the site plan only if it
materially and adversely affects Landlord's use of the reserved portions of the Property.

5.4 Construction Access. During construction, Tenant and its contractors shall have
unrestricted access to the Premises and easement areas twenty-four (24) hours per day,
seven (7) days per week.

5.5 Restoration of Construction Areas. Upon completion of construction, Tenant shall
restore all temporary construction areas, access routes, and staging areas to their
pre-construction condition, including reseeding with native grasses.

ARTICLE 6. OPERATIONS AND MAINTENANCE

6.1 Operation. Tenant shall operate and maintain the Solar Facility in a safe, efficient,
and professional manner, in compliance with all applicable laws, regulations, permits, and
interconnection agreements.

6.2 Vegetation Management. Tenant shall maintain appropriate vegetation management within
the Premises, including mowing and control of noxious weeds, in a manner that does not
adversely affect Landlord's agricultural operations on adjacent land.

6.3 Access Roads. Tenant shall maintain all access roads serving the Solar Facility in
good condition and shall repair any damage to Landlord's roads caused by Tenant's use.

6.4 Environmental Compliance. Tenant shall comply with all environmental laws and shall
not release or dispose of any hazardous materials on the Property. Tenant shall maintain
spill prevention and response plans for all petroleum products and chemicals used in
connection with the Solar Facility.

6.5 Noise and Visual Impact. Tenant shall design and operate the Solar Facility to minimize
noise and visual impact on surrounding properties and Landlord's residence.

ARTICLE 7. UTILITIES AND TAXES

7.1 Utilities. Tenant shall be responsible for all utility costs associated with the Solar
Facility.

7.2 Taxes. Tenant shall pay all real estate taxes, assessments, and other charges
attributable to the Premises and the Solar Facility. Tenant shall cooperate with Landlord
to ensure that taxes on the reserved portions of the Property are not adversely affected
by Tenant's use.

7.3 Tax Abatements. Any tax abatements, exemptions, or incentives related to the Solar
Facility shall inure solely to the benefit of Tenant.

ARTICLE 8. INSURANCE

8.1 Tenant's Insurance. Tenant shall maintain:
    (a) Commercial general liability: $5,000,000 per occurrence / $10,000,000 aggregate;
    (b) Property insurance on the Solar Facility at full replacement cost;
    (c) Builder's risk insurance during construction;
    (d) Workers' compensation as required by law;
    (e) Umbrella/excess liability: $10,000,000;
    (f) Environmental/pollution liability: $5,000,000.

8.2 Landlord as Additional Insured. All liability policies shall name Landlord as
additional insured.

ARTICLE 9. ASSIGNMENT AND FINANCING

9.1 Assignment. Tenant shall not assign this Lease without Landlord's prior written
consent, not to be unreasonably withheld; provided, however, that Tenant may, without
Landlord's consent, assign this Lease to: (a) any affiliate of Tenant; (b) any entity
acquiring all or substantially all of Tenant's assets; or (c) any lender providing
financing for the Solar Facility, as security for such financing.

9.2 Lender Protections. This Lease shall include customary protections for Tenant's
lenders, including: (a) the right to receive copies of all default notices; (b) the right
to cure defaults on Tenant's behalf; (c) step-in rights; and (d) a new lease in the
event of termination, upon substantially the same terms.

9.3 Estoppel. Upon request, each party shall execute and deliver estoppel certificates
within fifteen (15) business days.

ARTICLE 10. DEFAULT AND REMEDIES

10.1 Tenant Default. Events of default include: (a) failure to pay rent within thirty (30)
days of written notice; (b) failure to perform any other obligation within sixty (60) days
of written notice (or such longer period as reasonably necessary); (c) abandonment of the
Solar Facility for more than twelve (12) consecutive months; (d) bankruptcy or insolvency.

10.2 Landlord's Remedies. Upon default, Landlord may: (a) terminate this Lease upon one
hundred eighty (180) days' notice (to allow lender cure); (b) pursue damages; (c) exercise
self-help to cure environmental violations at Tenant's expense.

10.3 Landlord Default. If Landlord fails to perform any obligation within thirty (30) days
of written notice, Tenant may: (a) cure the default and offset costs against rent; (b)
terminate this Lease if the default materially impairs operations.

ARTICLE 11. DECOMMISSIONING

11.1 Obligation. Upon expiration or termination of this Lease, Tenant shall, at Tenant's
sole cost, decommission and remove the Solar Facility, including:
    (a) Removal of all solar panels, racking, inverters, transformers, and associated
        electrical equipment;
    (b) Removal of all foundations, pilings, and support structures to a depth of at
        least thirty-six (36) inches below grade;
    (c) Removal of all underground cables, conduits, and utilities;
    (d) Removal of all access roads, fencing, and ancillary structures;
    (e) Restoration of the Premises to a condition suitable for agricultural use,
        including regrading, topsoil restoration, and reseeding.

11.2 Timeline. Decommissioning shall be completed within eighteen (18) months following
the expiration or termination of this Lease.

11.3 Security. Tenant's decommissioning obligations are secured by the performance bond
or letter of credit described in Article 4.

ARTICLE 12. CONDEMNATION

12.1 If all or a material portion of the Premises is taken by eminent domain, either party
may terminate this Lease. Landlord shall receive the award for the value of the land, and
Tenant shall receive the award for the value of the Solar Facility improvements and
business losses.

ARTICLE 13. ENVIRONMENTAL INDEMNITY

13.1 Tenant shall indemnify and hold Landlord harmless from all claims, costs, damages,
and liabilities arising from environmental contamination caused by Tenant's activities.
This indemnity shall survive the expiration or termination of this Lease.

13.2 Landlord represents that, to the best of Landlord's knowledge, the Property is free
of environmental contamination as of the Commencement Date. Landlord shall indemnify Tenant
from claims arising from pre-existing contamination.

ARTICLE 14. MISCELLANEOUS

14.1 Governing Law. This Lease shall be governed by the laws of the State of Iowa.

14.2 Dispute Resolution. Disputes shall be submitted to mediation. If mediation fails,
disputes shall be resolved by binding arbitration in Des Moines, Iowa, under the rules of
the American Arbitration Association.

14.3 Quiet Enjoyment. Landlord covenants that Tenant shall peacefully enjoy the Premises
during the Lease Term, subject to the terms of this Lease.

14.4 Recording. The parties shall execute and record a Memorandum of Lease in the real
property records of Story County, Iowa.

14.5 Notices. All notices shall be sent by certified mail or nationally recognized
overnight courier to the addresses above.

14.6 Force Majeure. Neither party shall be liable for delays caused by events beyond
reasonable control, except the obligation to pay rent shall not be excused.

14.7 Entire Agreement. This Lease constitutes the entire agreement between the parties.

14.8 Amendments. No amendment shall be effective unless in writing and signed by both
parties.

14.9 Successors and Assigns. This Lease shall be binding upon and inure to the benefit
of the parties and their respective successors and permitted assigns.
