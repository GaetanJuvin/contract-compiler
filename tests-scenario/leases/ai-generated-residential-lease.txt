RESIDENTIAL LEASE AGREEMENT

THIS RESIDENTIAL LEASE AGREEMENT ("Agreement") is entered into as of the 1st day of
March, 2026, by and between:

LANDLORD: Greenfield Property Management, LLC, a Delaware limited liability company,
with its principal office at 4200 Maple Ridge Drive, Suite 300, Wilmington, DE 19801
("Landlord")

TENANT: Sarah J. Mitchell and David R. Mitchell, individuals residing at 78 Birchwood
Lane, Apt. 4B, Newark, DE 19711 ("Tenant")

WITNESSETH:

In consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, and for other
good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby
acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

SECTION 1. PREMISES

1.1 Landlord hereby leases to Tenant, and Tenant hereby leases from Landlord, the
residential property located at 1527 Oakwood Terrace, Unit 12A, Wilmington, DE 19802
(the "Premises"), consisting of approximately 1,450 square feet of living space, including
three (3) bedrooms, two (2) bathrooms, a kitchen, living room, and one (1) assigned
parking space designated as Space #42.

1.2 The Premises are leased for residential purposes only. Tenant shall not conduct any
business, commercial, or professional activity on the Premises without prior written
consent of Landlord.

SECTION 2. TERM

2.1 The initial term of this Lease shall commence on April 1, 2026, and shall expire on
March 31, 2027 (the "Initial Term"), unless sooner terminated in accordance with the
provisions of this Agreement.

2.2 Upon expiration of the Initial Term, this Lease shall automatically renew on a
month-to-month basis under the same terms and conditions, unless either party provides
written notice of non-renewal at least sixty (60) days prior to the expiration of the
then-current term.

SECTION 3. RENT

3.1 Tenant agrees to pay Landlord a monthly rent of Two Thousand Two Hundred Dollars
($2,200.00) per month during the Initial Term. Rent shall be due and payable on the first
(1st) day of each calendar month, without demand, deduction, or setoff.

3.2 If Tenant fails to pay rent by the fifth (5th) day of the month, a late fee of Five
Percent (5%) of the monthly rent shall be assessed, amounting to One Hundred Ten Dollars
($110.00).

3.3 Rent shall be paid by electronic funds transfer, certified check, or money order made
payable to "Greenfield Property Management, LLC" and delivered to the address specified in
Section 18 or to such other address as Landlord may designate in writing.

3.4 For the first year of occupancy, monthly rent shall be One Thousand Nine Hundred Fifty
Dollars ($1,950.00), payable in accordance with Section 3.1 above.

[ANOMALY: Section 3.1 states $2,200/month; Section 3.4 states $1,950/month for the first
year -- contradictory rent amounts]

SECTION 4. SECURITY DEPOSIT

4.1 Upon execution of this Agreement, Tenant shall deposit with Landlord the sum of Three
Thousand Three Hundred Dollars ($3,300.00) as a security deposit (the "Security Deposit").

4.2 The Security Deposit shall be held by Landlord in a separate, interest-bearing account
at a federally insured financial institution in accordance with Delaware Code Title 25,
Section 5514.

4.3 The Security Deposit may be applied by Landlord to remedy any default by Tenant,
including but not limited to unpaid rent, repair of damages beyond normal wear and tear,
and cleaning costs.

4.4 Within twenty (20) days after the termination of this Lease and Tenant's surrender of
the Premises, Landlord shall return the Security Deposit, less any lawful deductions, with
an itemized statement of deductions.

SECTION 5. UTILITIES AND SERVICES

5.1 Tenant shall be responsible for and shall promptly pay all charges for the following
utilities and services: electricity, gas, water, sewer, trash collection, internet, cable,
and telephone.

5.2 Landlord shall be responsible for maintaining common area lighting and landscaping of
the building grounds.

SECTION 6. USE AND OCCUPANCY

6.1 The Premises shall be occupied solely by the following persons: Sarah J. Mitchell,
David R. Mitchell, and their minor child, Emma Mitchell (age 7).

6.2 No additional persons shall reside on the Premises without the prior written consent
of Landlord. Guests staying longer than fourteen (14) consecutive days or more than thirty
(30) days in any twelve-month period shall be deemed unauthorized occupants.

6.3 Tenant shall not keep any pets on the Premises without prior written consent of
Landlord and payment of a non-refundable pet deposit of $500.00 per animal.

SECTION 7. MAINTENANCE AND REPAIRS

7.1 Landlord shall maintain the structural components of the building, including the roof,
exterior walls, foundation, and common areas, in good repair and in compliance with all
applicable building codes and health regulations.

7.2 Tenant shall maintain the interior of the Premises in clean, sanitary, and good
condition, and shall promptly notify Landlord of any condition requiring repair.

7.3 Tenant shall be responsible for repairs necessitated by Tenant's misuse, waste, or
neglect, or that of Tenant's family, guests, or invitees.

7.4 Landlord shall respond to emergency maintenance requests within twenty-four (24)
hours and non-emergency requests within seventy-two (72) hours.

SECTION 8. ALTERATIONS AND IMPROVEMENTS

8.1 Tenant shall not make any alterations, additions, or improvements to the Premises
without the prior written consent of Landlord, which shall not be unreasonably withheld.

8.2 Any approved alterations shall be performed in a workmanlike manner and in compliance
with all applicable laws, regulations, and building codes.

8.3 Unless otherwise agreed in writing, all alterations, additions, and improvements shall
become the property of Landlord upon installation.

SECTION 9. INSURANCE

9.1 Tenant shall obtain and maintain, at Tenant's sole expense, a renter's insurance
policy with minimum coverage of $100,000 for personal property and $300,000 for personal
liability, naming Landlord as an additional interested party.

9.2 Landlord shall maintain property insurance covering the structure and common areas of
the building. Landlord's insurance shall not cover Tenant's personal property.

SECTION 10. ENTRY BY LANDLORD

10.1 Landlord or Landlord's agents may enter the Premises at reasonable times upon at
least twenty-four (24) hours' prior written or electronic notice to Tenant for the
following purposes: inspection, repairs, showing the Premises to prospective tenants or
purchasers, or as otherwise permitted by law.

10.2 In the event of an emergency, Landlord may enter the Premises without prior notice.

SECTION 11. ASSIGNMENT AND SUBLETTING

11.1 Tenant shall not assign this Lease or sublet the Premises, or any part thereof,
without the prior written consent of Landlord, which consent shall not be unreasonably
withheld, conditioned, or delayed.

11.2 Any approved assignment or subletting shall not release Tenant from Tenant's
obligations under this Lease.

SECTION 12. DEFAULT AND REMEDIES

12.1 The following shall constitute events of default by Tenant: (a) failure to pay rent
or other charges within ten (10) days after written notice from Landlord; (b) violation
of any other term of this Lease that is not cured within thirty (30) days after written
notice from Landlord; (c) abandonment of the Premises; (d) filing of a petition in
bankruptcy by or against Tenant.

12.2 Upon the occurrence of an event of default, Landlord may, at Landlord's option:
(a) terminate this Lease upon fifteen (15) days' written notice; (b) re-enter and take
possession of the Premises; (c) pursue any other remedy available at law or in equity.

12.3 Tenant shall be liable for all costs and expenses incurred by Landlord in enforcing
this Lease, including reasonable attorney's fees and court costs.

SECTION 13. TERMINATION AND SURRENDER

13.1 Upon the expiration or earlier termination of this Lease, Tenant shall surrender the
Premises in the same condition as received, ordinary wear and tear excepted.

13.2 Any personal property remaining on the Premises after the termination date shall be
deemed abandoned and may be disposed of by Landlord at Tenant's expense.

SECTION 14. QUIET ENJOYMENT

14.1 Landlord covenants that Tenant, upon paying the rent and performing the covenants
herein, shall peacefully and quietly hold, occupy, and enjoy the Premises during the term
of this Lease without hindrance from Landlord or any person claiming through Landlord.

SECTION 15. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

15.1 This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the
State of Delaware.

15.2 Any dispute arising under this Agreement shall first be submitted to mediation
administered by the American Arbitration Association. If mediation is unsuccessful, the
dispute shall be resolved by binding arbitration in New Castle County, Delaware.

SECTION 16. LEAD-BASED PAINT DISCLOSURE

16.1 For properties built prior to 1978: Landlord has disclosed the known presence of
lead-based paint or lead-based paint hazards in accordance with 42 U.S.C. 4852d and has
provided Tenant with the EPA pamphlet "Protect Your Family From Lead in Your Home."

16.2 The property was constructed in 2004; therefore, lead-based paint disclosure is not
applicable.

SECTION 17. NOTICES

17.1 All notices required or permitted under this Lease shall be in writing and shall be
deemed given when delivered personally, sent by certified mail (return receipt requested),
or sent by recognized overnight courier to the addresses set forth above or to such other
address as a party may designate in writing.

SECTION 18. ENTIRE AGREEMENT

18.1 This Agreement, together with any addenda attached hereto, constitutes the entire
agreement between the parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all
prior negotiations, representations, warranties, commitments, offers, and agreements,
whether written or oral.

18.2 This Agreement may not be modified or amended except by a written instrument signed
by both parties.

SECTION 19. SEVERABILITY

19.1 If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable,
the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

SECTION 20. SIGNATURES
