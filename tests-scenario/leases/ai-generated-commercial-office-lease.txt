COMMERCIAL OFFICE LEASE AGREEMENT

THIS COMMERCIAL OFFICE LEASE AGREEMENT ("Lease") is made and entered into effective as
of the 15th day of February, 2026, by and between:

LANDLORD: Pinnacle Tower Holdings, Inc., a New York corporation, with its principal
office at 1 World Trade Center, Suite 8500, New York, NY 10007 ("Landlord")

TENANT: Meridian Consulting Group, LLC, a New York limited liability company, with its
principal office at 380 Lexington Avenue, 17th Floor, New York, NY 10168 ("Tenant")

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Landlord is the owner of that certain commercial office building known as
Pinnacle Tower, located at 500 Park Avenue, New York, NY 10065 (the "Building"); and

WHEREAS, Tenant desires to lease certain office space within the Building for the conduct
of its professional consulting business; and

WHEREAS, Landlord desires to lease such space to Tenant upon the terms and conditions set
forth herein;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein and for other
good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby
acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

ARTICLE 1. PREMISES

1.1 Demised Premises. Landlord hereby leases to Tenant, and Tenant hereby leases from
Landlord, approximately 8,500 rentable square feet located on the 22nd floor of the
Building, as more particularly described on Exhibit A attached hereto and incorporated
herein by reference (the "Premises").

1.2 Common Areas. Tenant shall have the non-exclusive right to use, in common with other
tenants and occupants of the Building, the lobbies, corridors, elevators, stairways,
restrooms on multi-tenant floors, and other common areas of the Building.

1.3 Parking. Landlord shall make available to Tenant up to five (5) unreserved parking
spaces in the Building's underground parking garage at the prevailing monthly rate, which
is currently $450.00 per space per month.

ARTICLE 2. TERM

2.1 Lease Term. The term of this Lease ("Lease Term") shall be seven (7) years, commencing
on May 1, 2026 ("Commencement Date"), and expiring on April 30, 2033, unless sooner
terminated or extended as provided herein.

2.2 Renewal Option. Tenant shall have the option to renew this Lease for one (1) additional
term of five (5) years ("Renewal Term") by providing written notice to Landlord not less
than twelve (12) months prior to the expiration of the initial Lease Term. The Renewal Term
shall be upon the same terms and conditions as the initial Lease Term, except that the Base
Rent shall be adjusted to the then-prevailing fair market rental rate for comparable office
space in Midtown Manhattan, as determined pursuant to Section 3.3.

2.3 Early Access. Landlord shall provide Tenant with access to the Premises thirty (30)
days prior to the Commencement Date for the sole purpose of installing furniture, fixtures,
telecommunications equipment, and other personal property. No rent shall be due during this
early access period.

ARTICLE 3. RENT

3.1 Base Rent. Tenant agrees to pay Landlord annual Base Rent as follows:

    Lease Years 1-3:   $76.00 per rentable square foot per annum ($646,000.00 annually;
                        $53,833.33 per month)
    Lease Years 4-5:   $82.00 per rentable square foot per annum ($697,000.00 annually;
                        $58,083.33 per month)
    Lease Years 6-7:   $88.00 per rentable square foot per annum ($748,000.00 annually;
                        $62,333.33 per month)

3.2 Additional Rent. In addition to Base Rent, Tenant shall pay as Additional Rent
Tenant's Proportionate Share of Operating Expenses and Real Estate Taxes in excess of the
Base Year amounts, as further described in Article 5.

3.3 Fair Market Rent Determination. If the parties cannot agree on the fair market rental
rate for any Renewal Term within sixty (60) days after Tenant's renewal notice, the rate
shall be determined by a panel of three (3) licensed commercial real estate appraisers,
one selected by each party and the third selected by the first two appraisers. The fair
market rate shall be the average of the two closest appraisals.

3.4 Payment Terms. Base Rent and Additional Rent shall be payable in advance on the first
(1st) business day of each calendar month, without notice, demand, abatement, deduction,
or setoff. All payments shall be made by wire transfer to the account designated by
Landlord.

3.5 Late Payment. If any installment of Rent is not received by Landlord within five (5)
business days after the due date, Tenant shall pay a late charge equal to four percent (4%)
of the overdue amount, plus interest at the rate of one and one-half percent (1.5%) per
month on the unpaid balance from the due date until paid.

ARTICLE 4. SECURITY DEPOSIT

4.1 Deposit Amount. Upon execution of this Lease, Tenant shall deliver to Landlord a
security deposit in the amount of One Hundred Sixty-One Thousand Five Hundred Dollars
($161,500.00), representing three (3) months of initial Base Rent (the "Security Deposit").

4.2 Letter of Credit Alternative. In lieu of a cash Security Deposit, Tenant may deliver
to Landlord an irrevocable standby letter of credit issued by a nationally recognized
commercial bank acceptable to Landlord, in the amount and for the term specified herein.

4.3 Application. Landlord may apply all or any portion of the Security Deposit to cure any
default by Tenant. In such event, Tenant shall promptly restore the Security Deposit to
its original amount within ten (10) business days after written notice from Landlord.

4.4 Return. The Security Deposit shall be returned to Tenant within forty-five (45) days
following the later of (a) the expiration or termination of this Lease, and (b) Tenant's
complete vacation and surrender of the Premises, less any amounts applied in accordance
with this Article.

ARTICLE 5. OPERATING EXPENSES AND TAXES

5.1 Base Year. The "Base Year" for Operating Expenses and Real Estate Taxes shall be
calendar year 2026.

5.2 Tenant's Proportionate Share. Tenant's Proportionate Share shall be 4.72%, calculated
by dividing the rentable square footage of the Premises (8,500 RSF) by the total rentable
square footage of the Building (180,000 RSF).

5.3 Operating Expense Escalation. Beginning January 1, 2027, Tenant shall pay Tenant's
Proportionate Share of the increase in Operating Expenses over the Base Year amount.
Landlord shall provide Tenant with an annual estimate of Operating Expenses, and Tenant
shall pay one-twelfth (1/12) of Tenant's estimated share monthly with Base Rent.

5.4 Reconciliation. Within one hundred twenty (120) days after the end of each calendar
year, Landlord shall provide Tenant with a statement of actual Operating Expenses and Real
Estate Taxes. If Tenant's payments exceed Tenant's actual share, Landlord shall credit the
overpayment against the next installment(s) of Additional Rent. If Tenant's payments are
less than Tenant's actual share, Tenant shall pay the deficiency within thirty (30) days
after receipt of Landlord's statement.

5.5 Audit Right. Tenant shall have the right, at Tenant's expense, to audit Landlord's
books and records relating to Operating Expenses for any calendar year, provided such audit
is commenced within twelve (12) months after receipt of Landlord's annual statement.

ARTICLE 6. USE

6.1 Permitted Use. The Premises shall be used and occupied solely for general office
purposes, including management consulting, financial advisory services, and related
professional activities.

6.2 Compliance with Laws. Tenant shall comply with all applicable federal, state, and
local laws, ordinances, rules, and regulations in connection with Tenant's use and
occupancy of the Premises.

6.3 Prohibited Uses. Tenant shall not use or permit the use of the Premises for any
purpose that is unlawful, hazardous, offensive, or that would increase the cost of
insurance for the Building or create a nuisance.

ARTICLE 7. TENANT IMPROVEMENTS

7.1 Tenant Improvement Allowance. Landlord shall provide Tenant with a tenant improvement
allowance of $65.00 per rentable square foot ($552,500.00) (the "TI Allowance") for the
design and construction of initial tenant improvements to the Premises.

7.2 Construction. Tenant improvements shall be designed by an architect approved by
Landlord and constructed by a general contractor approved by Landlord, such approvals not
to be unreasonably withheld. All improvements shall comply with the Building's standards
and specifications.

7.3 Excess Costs. Any costs in excess of the TI Allowance shall be the sole responsibility
of Tenant.

ARTICLE 8. MAINTENANCE AND REPAIRS

8.1 Landlord's Obligations. Landlord shall maintain in good order and repair: (a) the
structural elements of the Building, including the roof, foundation, exterior walls, and
load-bearing walls; (b) the Building's mechanical, electrical, plumbing, HVAC, elevator,
and life safety systems; (c) common areas; and (d) the exterior of the Building and
surrounding grounds.

8.2 Tenant's Obligations. Tenant shall maintain the interior of the Premises in good order,
condition, and repair, including all non-structural elements, interior walls, flooring,
ceiling tiles, light fixtures, and supplemental HVAC units exclusively serving the Premises.

ARTICLE 9. INSURANCE

9.1 Tenant's Insurance. Tenant shall maintain, at Tenant's sole expense, the following
insurance coverages: (a) commercial general liability insurance with limits of not less
than $2,000,000 per occurrence and $5,000,000 in the aggregate; (b) property insurance
covering Tenant's personal property and improvements; (c) workers' compensation insurance
as required by law; and (d) business interruption insurance covering not less than twelve
(12) months of Rent.

9.2 Landlord's Insurance. Landlord shall maintain property insurance on the Building,
commercial general liability insurance, and such other insurance as Landlord deems
appropriate or as may be required by Landlord's lender.

9.3 Waiver of Subrogation. Landlord and Tenant each waive any right of recovery against
the other for any loss or damage covered by their respective insurance policies, and each
shall cause their insurers to issue appropriate waiver of subrogation endorsements.

ARTICLE 10. ASSIGNMENT AND SUBLETTING

10.1 Consent Required. Tenant shall not assign this Lease or sublet all or any portion of
the Premises without the prior written consent of Landlord, which consent shall not be
unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed.

10.2 Recapture Right. In the event Tenant requests consent to an assignment or subletting,
Landlord shall have the right, exercisable within thirty (30) days after receipt of
Tenant's request, to recapture the space that is the subject of the proposed transaction
by terminating this Lease (in the case of an assignment or subletting of the entire
Premises) or by terminating this Lease with respect to the space proposed to be sublet.

10.3 Profit Sharing. If Tenant assigns or sublets the Premises at a rental rate exceeding
the Rent payable hereunder, Tenant shall pay to Landlord fifty percent (50%) of such
excess, after deducting Tenant's reasonable costs of the transaction.

ARTICLE 11. INDEMNIFICATION

11.1 Tenant's Indemnification. Tenant shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Landlord
and its officers, directors, employees, agents, and contractors from and against any and
all claims, damages, losses, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees)
arising out of or related to Tenant's use or occupancy of the Premises, except to the
extent caused by Landlord's negligence or willful misconduct.

11.2 Landlord's Indemnification. Landlord shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Tenant
from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, costs, and expenses arising out of
Landlord's negligence or willful misconduct in the operation and maintenance of the Building.

ARTICLE 12. DEFAULT AND REMEDIES

12.1 Tenant Default. Each of the following shall constitute an event of default by Tenant:
(a) failure to pay Rent within ten (10) days after written notice; (b) failure to perform
any other covenant within thirty (30) days after written notice (or such longer period as
may be reasonably required to cure, provided Tenant commences the cure within such thirty-day
period and diligently pursues it to completion); (c) assignment for the benefit of creditors;
(d) filing of a petition in bankruptcy.

12.2 Landlord's Remedies. Upon the occurrence of a Tenant default, Landlord may:
(a) terminate this Lease; (b) re-enter and repossess the Premises; (c) accelerate all Rent
for the remainder of the Lease Term; (d) pursue any other remedy available at law or equity.

12.3 Landlord Default. Landlord shall be in default if Landlord fails to perform any
obligation under this Lease within thirty (30) days after written notice from Tenant (or
such longer period as may be reasonably required, provided Landlord commences cure within
such period).

ARTICLE 13. CASUALTY AND CONDEMNATION

13.1 Damage or Destruction. If the Premises or the Building are damaged by fire or other
casualty, Landlord shall notify Tenant within sixty (60) days of the estimated time to
restore. If restoration is estimated to take more than two hundred seventy (270) days,
either party may terminate this Lease upon thirty (30) days' written notice.

13.2 Rent Abatement. Rent shall be abated in proportion to the portion of the Premises
rendered unusable during the period of restoration.

13.3 Condemnation. If all or a material portion of the Premises is taken by eminent domain,
either party may terminate this Lease. Landlord shall be entitled to all condemnation
awards, except that Tenant may separately claim compensation for its moving expenses and
loss of business.

ARTICLE 14. SUBORDINATION AND ESTOPPEL

14.1 Subordination. This Lease is subject and subordinate to all present and future
mortgages and deeds of trust affecting the Building, provided that the holder thereof
agrees to a non-disturbance agreement in favor of Tenant on commercially reasonable terms.

14.2 Estoppel Certificates. Each party agrees, within fifteen (15) business days after
request, to execute and deliver an estoppel certificate confirming the status of this Lease.

ARTICLE 15. SURRENDER

15.1 Condition. Upon expiration or termination, Tenant shall surrender the Premises in
broom-clean condition and in good order and repair, ordinary wear and tear excepted. Tenant
shall remove all personal property and, at Landlord's election, any alterations or
improvements designated by Landlord for removal.

ARTICLE 16. MISCELLANEOUS

16.1 Governing Law. This Lease shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the
laws of the State of New York, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

16.2 Entire Agreement. This Lease, together with all exhibits and addenda attached hereto,
constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior negotiations
and agreements.

16.3 Amendments. This Lease may not be modified except by a written instrument signed by
both parties.

16.4 Notices. All notices shall be in writing and shall be deemed given when delivered
personally, sent by certified mail (return receipt requested), or sent by nationally
recognized overnight courier to the addresses set forth above.

16.5 Broker. Landlord and Tenant each represent that neither has dealt with any real estate
broker in connection with this Lease except CBRE, Inc. (representing Tenant) and JLL
(representing Landlord). Landlord shall pay all brokerage commissions.

16.6 Counterparts. This Lease may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be
deemed an original.
