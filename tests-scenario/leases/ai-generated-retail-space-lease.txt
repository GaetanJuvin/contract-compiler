RETAIL SPACE LEASE AGREEMENT

THIS RETAIL SPACE LEASE AGREEMENT ("Lease") is made effective as of the 10th day of
January, 2026, by and between:

LANDLORD: Crossroads Mall Partners, LP, a California limited partnership, with its
principal office at 9100 Wilshire Boulevard, Suite 700, Beverly Hills, CA 90212
("Landlord")

TENANT: Bloom & Vine Boutique, Inc., a California corporation, with its registered
office at 2245 Pacific Coast Highway, Malibu, CA 90265 ("Tenant")

RECITALS

Landlord is the owner and operator of the commercial retail center known as Crossroads
Shopping Center, located at 18500 Ventura Boulevard, Tarzana, CA 91356 (the "Shopping
Center"). Tenant desires to lease retail space within the Shopping Center for the
operation of a specialty women's clothing and accessories boutique.

AGREEMENT

ARTICLE 1. PREMISES AND COMMON AREAS

1.1 Premises. Landlord leases to Tenant and Tenant leases from Landlord the retail space
designated as Suite 214 within the Shopping Center, containing approximately 2,800 square
feet of gross leasable area, as depicted on Exhibit A (the "Premises").

1.2 Common Areas. The "Common Areas" include all areas within the Shopping Center
designated by Landlord for the common use of tenants and their customers, including
parking areas, sidewalks, landscaped areas, service corridors, public restrooms, and
any food court areas. Landlord reserves the right to modify Common Areas from time to time.

1.3 Tenant's Share. Tenant's pro rata share of the Shopping Center for purposes of
Common Area Maintenance charges shall be 1.87%, based on the ratio of the Premises
(2,800 sq. ft.) to the total gross leasable area of the Shopping Center (149,700 sq. ft.).

ARTICLE 2. TERM

2.1 Initial Term. The Lease Term shall be five (5) years, commencing on April 1, 2026
("Commencement Date"), and expiring on March 31, 2031.

2.2 Renewal Options. Tenant shall have two (2) options to renew this Lease for successive
periods of three (3) years each, upon not less than one hundred eighty (180) days' prior
written notice. Renewal terms shall be at the then-prevailing market rate but not less than
the rent in effect during the last year of the preceding term plus three percent (3%).

2.3 Kick-Out Clause. If Tenant's gross sales from the Premises do not exceed $600,000 in
any consecutive twelve-month period during the first three (3) years of the Lease Term,
Tenant may terminate this Lease upon ninety (90) days' prior written notice, subject to
payment of an early termination fee equal to three (3) months' Base Rent.

ARTICLE 3. RENT

3.1 Base Rent. Tenant shall pay Base Rent as follows:

    Year 1:   $14.00 per square foot per annum ($39,200.00/year; $3,266.67/month)
    Year 2:   $14.56 per square foot per annum ($40,768.00/year; $3,397.33/month)
    Year 3:   $15.14 per square foot per annum ($42,392.00/year; $3,532.67/month)
    Year 4:   $15.75 per square foot per annum ($44,100.00/year; $3,675.00/month)
    Year 5:   $16.38 per square foot per annum ($45,864.00/year; $3,822.00/month)

3.2 Percentage Rent. In addition to Base Rent, Tenant shall pay Percentage Rent equal to
six percent (6%) of Tenant's annual Gross Sales in excess of the "Breakpoint" for each
Lease Year. The Breakpoint for Year 1 shall be $653,333.33, and shall be adjusted
proportionally for subsequent years based on the Base Rent increase.

3.3 Common Area Maintenance (CAM). Tenant shall pay monthly, as Additional Rent, Tenant's
proportionate share of Common Area Maintenance expenses, currently estimated at $4.25 per
square foot per annum ($11,900.00/year; $991.67/month), subject to annual reconciliation.

3.4 Payment. All Rent shall be due on the first (1st) day of each calendar month, payable
to Landlord at the address specified in Article 16 or by electronic payment as directed by
Landlord. If the Commencement Date falls other than on the first day of a month, rent for
the partial month shall be prorated.

3.5 Late Charges. If any payment of Rent is not received within ten (10) days after the
due date, Tenant shall pay a late charge of $250.00 plus interest on the overdue amount at
the rate of twelve percent (12%) per annum.

ARTICLE 4. GROSS SALES REPORTING

4.1 Monthly Reports. Tenant shall furnish to Landlord, within fifteen (15) days after
the end of each calendar month, a written statement of Tenant's Gross Sales for the
preceding month.

4.2 Annual Statement. Within ninety (90) days after the end of each Lease Year, Tenant
shall furnish Landlord a statement of annual Gross Sales, certified by Tenant's chief
financial officer or independent certified public accountant.

4.3 Records. Tenant shall keep accurate books and records of Gross Sales at the Premises
or at Tenant's principal office for a period of not less than three (3) years. Landlord
shall have the right to audit such records at reasonable times upon ten (10) days' notice.

4.4 Definition of Gross Sales. "Gross Sales" means the total amount of all sales of
merchandise and services made from, at, or through the Premises, whether for cash or
credit, including online orders fulfilled from the Premises, after deducting returns,
allowances, and sales taxes collected and remitted to taxing authorities.

ARTICLE 5. USE AND OPERATION

5.1 Permitted Use. The Premises shall be used solely for the operation of a retail
women's clothing and accessories boutique under the trade name "Bloom & Vine" or such
other name as Landlord may approve in writing.

5.2 Continuous Operation. Tenant shall continuously operate its business in the entire
Premises during the Lease Term, maintaining regular business hours of not less than
10:00 AM to 8:00 PM Monday through Saturday, and 11:00 AM to 6:00 PM on Sundays, or
such other hours as may be established by Landlord for the Shopping Center.

5.3 Exclusive Use. Landlord shall not lease space in the Shopping Center to any other
tenant whose primary business is the retail sale of women's clothing and accessories,
provided that this restriction shall not apply to department stores or tenants occupying
more than 10,000 square feet.

5.4 Radius Restriction. During the Lease Term, Tenant shall not directly or indirectly
own, operate, or have an interest in any similar retail business within a five (5) mile
radius of the Shopping Center without Landlord's prior written consent.

ARTICLE 6. MAINTENANCE AND REPAIRS

6.1 Tenant's Obligations. Tenant shall, at Tenant's sole cost and expense, maintain the
interior of the Premises in good condition, including all fixtures, equipment, interior
walls, floors, ceilings, plate glass, doors, signage, plumbing fixtures, and electrical
fixtures within the Premises.

6.2 Landlord's Obligations. Landlord shall maintain the structural components of the
Shopping Center, the roof, exterior walls, parking areas, landscaping, and Common Areas
in good condition and repair.

6.3 HVAC. Tenant shall be responsible for the maintenance, repair, and replacement of
any HVAC equipment exclusively serving the Premises. Tenant shall maintain a preventive
maintenance contract with a licensed HVAC contractor approved by Landlord.

ARTICLE 7. ALTERATIONS

7.1 Initial Build-Out. Landlord shall deliver the Premises in "vanilla shell" condition.
Tenant shall be responsible for all interior build-out at Tenant's sole expense.

7.2 Landlord's Contribution. Landlord shall provide a tenant improvement allowance of
$25.00 per square foot ($70,000.00) toward Tenant's initial build-out costs.

7.3 Subsequent Alterations. Tenant shall not make any alterations to the Premises costing
in excess of $10,000.00 without the prior written consent of Landlord.

ARTICLE 8. SIGNAGE

8.1 Storefront Signage. Tenant shall install, at Tenant's expense, an illuminated sign on
the storefront of the Premises in accordance with the Shopping Center's sign criteria as
set forth in Exhibit C.

8.2 Directory Listing. Landlord shall include Tenant's name in all Shopping Center
directories and wayfinding signage at no additional charge.

8.3 Pylon Signage. Subject to availability, Tenant may obtain space on the Shopping
Center's pylon sign at a monthly charge determined by Landlord.

ARTICLE 9. INSURANCE

9.1 Tenant's Insurance. Tenant shall maintain: (a) commercial general liability insurance
with limits of not less than $1,000,000 per occurrence and $2,000,000 aggregate; (b) all-
risk property insurance covering Tenant's inventory, fixtures, and improvements; (c)
workers' compensation as required by law; and (d) business interruption insurance.

9.2 Landlord as Additional Insured. All liability policies shall name Landlord, its
partners, and its managing agent as additional insureds.

9.3 Waiver of Subrogation. Landlord and Tenant mutually waive all rights of subrogation
against each other.

ARTICLE 10. ASSIGNMENT AND SUBLETTING

10.1 Landlord Consent Required. Tenant may not assign this Lease or sublet the Premises
without Landlord's prior written consent, which may be withheld in Landlord's sole and
absolute discretion.

[ANOMALY: Typical commercial leases require consent "not to be unreasonably withheld" --
this gives landlord absolute discretion, an asymmetric term]

10.2 No Release. Any permitted assignment shall not release Tenant from its obligations
under this Lease.

ARTICLE 11. DEFAULT AND REMEDIES

11.1 Tenant Default. The occurrence of any of the following shall constitute a default:
(a) failure to pay Rent within five (5) days of the due date; (b) failure to perform any
other obligation within fifteen (15) days after written notice; (c) abandonment or failure
to continuously operate; (d) insolvency or bankruptcy.

11.2 Remedies. Upon a Tenant default, Landlord may: (a) terminate this Lease and recover
damages including the worth at the time of award of the unpaid rent for the balance of
the term discounted at the rate of the Federal Reserve Bank of San Francisco plus one
percent; (b) continue the Lease in effect and collect rent as it becomes due (California
Civil Code Section 1951.4); (c) pursue any other remedy at law or equity.

11.3 Landlord Default. Landlord shall not be deemed in default unless Landlord fails to
cure within thirty (30) days after written notice from Tenant.

ARTICLE 12. DAMAGE AND DESTRUCTION

12.1 Repair Obligation. If the Premises are damaged by fire or other casualty, Landlord
shall repair the same with reasonable diligence, provided that if the damage exceeds fifty
percent (50%) of the replacement value of the Premises, Landlord may elect to terminate
this Lease upon thirty (30) days' notice.

12.2 Rent Abatement. Rent shall be abated in proportion to the area of the Premises
rendered unusable.

ARTICLE 13. ENVIRONMENTAL

13.1 Hazardous Materials. Tenant shall not bring, use, store, or dispose of any Hazardous
Materials on the Premises, except for commercially reasonable quantities of cleaning
supplies used in the ordinary course of Tenant's business.

13.2 Indemnity. Tenant shall indemnify Landlord from any claims arising from Tenant's use
of Hazardous Materials.

ARTICLE 14. SURRENDER AND HOLDOVER

14.1 Surrender. Upon expiration, Tenant shall surrender the Premises in good condition,
ordinary wear and tear excepted, and shall remove all personal property and trade fixtures.

14.2 Holdover. If Tenant holds over after expiration without Landlord's consent, Tenant
shall be deemed a tenant at sufferance at one hundred fifty percent (150%) of the last
monthly Rent, and Landlord may pursue eviction.

ARTICLE 15. INDEMNIFICATION

15.1 Tenant shall indemnify and hold harmless Landlord from all claims arising from
Tenant's use of the Premises or any act or omission of Tenant, its employees, agents, or
customers.

15.2 Landlord shall indemnify and hold harmless Tenant from all claims arising from
Landlord's negligence in maintaining the Common Areas and the structural components of
the Shopping Center.

ARTICLE 16. NOTICES

ARTICLE 17. MISCELLANEOUS

17.1 Governing Law. This Lease shall be governed by the laws of the State of California.

17.2 Entire Agreement. This Lease constitutes the entire agreement between the parties.

17.3 Severability. If any provision is held invalid, the remaining provisions shall
continue in full force and effect.

17.4 Attorneys' Fees. In any action to enforce this Lease, the prevailing party shall be
entitled to recover reasonable attorneys' fees.

17.5 Time of Essence. Time is of the essence with respect to all provisions of this Lease.
