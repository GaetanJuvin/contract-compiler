EXCLUSIVE DISTRIBUTION AGREEMENT

Agreement No.: DA-2025-0756
Effective Date: August 1, 2025

This Exclusive Distribution Agreement ("Agreement") is made by and between:

MANUFACTURER: Clearwater Beverage Company, LLC, a Florida limited liability company, with its principal office at 901 Bayshore Drive, Tampa, FL 33606 ("Manufacturer")

DISTRIBUTOR: National Beverage Distribution, Inc., a Georgia corporation, with its principal office at 4500 Peachtree Industrial Boulevard, Atlanta, GA 30341 ("Distributor")

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Manufacturer produces and markets a line of premium natural spring water, flavored sparkling water, and enhanced hydration beverages under the "ClearSpring" brand; and

WHEREAS, Manufacturer desires to appoint Distributor as its exclusive distributor for the Territory defined herein, and Distributor desires to accept such appointment;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual agreements set forth below, the Parties agree as follows:

ARTICLE 1 - APPOINTMENT AND TERRITORY

1.1 Exclusive Appointment. Manufacturer hereby appoints Distributor as its exclusive distributor of the Products (as defined below) within the Territory, and Distributor accepts such appointment. During the Term, Manufacturer shall not sell Products directly or through any other distributor within the Territory.

1.2 Territory. The "Territory" shall consist of the states of Georgia, Alabama, Tennessee, South Carolina, and North Carolina.

1.3 Products. "Products" means all beverages manufactured, marketed, or sold by Manufacturer under the ClearSpring brand, including all current SKUs listed in Exhibit A and any new SKUs introduced during the Term.

1.4 Reserved Rights. Manufacturer reserves the right to sell Products directly to national accounts whose headquarters are outside the Territory, even if such accounts have retail locations within the Territory, provided that Manufacturer shall pay Distributor a commission of three percent (3%) on all such direct sales delivered within the Territory.

ARTICLE 2 - DISTRIBUTOR OBLIGATIONS

2.1 Sales and Distribution. Distributor shall use its best commercial efforts to promote, market, and distribute the Products throughout the Territory. Distributor shall maintain a sales force and distribution network adequate to achieve the sales targets set forth in Exhibit B.

2.2 Minimum Purchase Requirements. Distributor shall purchase from Manufacturer no less than the minimum quantities set forth in Exhibit B for each contract year ("Minimum Purchase Requirement"). Failure to meet the Minimum Purchase Requirement in any contract year shall entitle Manufacturer to terminate the exclusivity granted hereunder or terminate this Agreement in accordance with Article 9.

2.3 Marketing. Distributor shall allocate no less than two percent (2%) of its annual gross revenues from the Products toward local marketing, advertising, and promotional activities. Distributor shall submit quarterly marketing reports to Manufacturer detailing activities and expenditures.

2.4 Warehousing and Storage. Distributor shall maintain temperature-controlled warehouse facilities adequate to preserve the quality and shelf life of the Products. Storage conditions shall comply with FDA regulations and Manufacturer's storage guidelines as set forth in Exhibit C.

2.5 Delivery and Service. Distributor shall provide regular delivery service to all retail accounts within the Territory and shall maintain service levels consistent with industry standards, including but not limited to: (a) delivery within forty-eight (48) hours of order placement for in-stock items; (b) fill rate of at least ninety-five percent (95%); and (c) order accuracy rate of at least ninety-nine percent (99%).

2.6 Reporting. Distributor shall provide Manufacturer with monthly sales reports, including: (a) unit and dollar sales by SKU; (b) inventory levels; (c) new account openings; (d) lost accounts; and (e) competitive activity observations.

ARTICLE 3 - MANUFACTURER OBLIGATIONS

3.1 Supply. Manufacturer shall supply Products to Distributor in quantities sufficient to meet Distributor's reasonable requirements, consistent with the forecasts provided under Section 4.1.

3.2 Product Quality. Manufacturer shall ensure that all Products meet applicable quality standards, are properly labeled, and comply with all federal, state, and local laws and regulations, including FDA requirements for food and beverage products.

3.3 Marketing Support. Manufacturer shall provide Distributor with point-of-sale materials, product samples, and brand guidelines. Manufacturer shall also contribute to co-operative advertising at a rate of one percent (1%) of Distributor's net purchases, subject to prior approval of advertising content.

3.4 New Product Introduction. Manufacturer shall provide Distributor with at least sixty (60) days' advance notice of any new Product launches and shall provide sufficient initial inventory and marketing materials to support the launch.

3.5 Training. Manufacturer shall provide product knowledge training to Distributor's sales team at least twice per year, at Manufacturer's expense.

ARTICLE 4 - ORDERING AND DELIVERY

4.1 Forecasts. Distributor shall provide Manufacturer with a rolling six (6) month demand forecast, updated monthly. Forecasts shall be non-binding but shall be prepared in good faith.

4.2 Purchase Orders. Distributor shall submit purchase orders at least fourteen (14) days prior to the requested delivery date. Purchase orders shall specify Products, quantities, delivery dates, and ship-to locations.

4.3 Order Acceptance. Manufacturer shall accept or reject purchase orders within three (3) business days. Orders consistent with the forecast shall not be unreasonably rejected.

4.4 Delivery. Manufacturer shall deliver Products FOB Manufacturer's distribution center in Tampa, Florida. Title and risk of loss shall pass to Distributor upon delivery to the common carrier at Manufacturer's facility.

4.5 Shelf Life. All Products delivered to Distributor shall have a minimum remaining shelf life of seventy-five percent (75%) of the total shelf life at the time of delivery.

ARTICLE 5 - PRICING AND PAYMENT

5.1 Wholesale Prices. The wholesale prices for Products are set forth in Exhibit A. Prices are exclusive of taxes and transportation costs.

5.2 Price Changes. Manufacturer shall provide at least sixty (60) days' written notice of any price changes. Price increases shall not exceed four percent (4%) per annum, unless due to documented extraordinary cost increases.

5.3 Payment Terms. Distributor shall pay all invoices within thirty (30) days of invoice date, net of any applicable credits or allowances.

5.4 Promotional Allowances. Manufacturer shall offer quarterly promotional pricing as set forth in Exhibit D. Promotional terms shall be communicated at least thirty (30) days in advance.

ARTICLE 6 - PRODUCT RECALLS AND LIABILITY

6.1 Recall Cooperation. In the event of a product recall, Distributor shall cooperate fully with Manufacturer and shall assist in identifying and recovering recalled Products from the distribution channel. Manufacturer shall bear all costs of the recall, including Distributor's reasonable out-of-pocket expenses.

6.2 Product Liability. Manufacturer shall maintain product liability insurance with limits of no less than Five Million Dollars ($5,000,000) per occurrence and shall name Distributor as an additional insured.

6.3 Indemnification. Manufacturer shall indemnify Distributor against all claims arising from product defects, contamination, mislabeling, or failure to comply with applicable regulations. Distributor shall indemnify Manufacturer against claims arising from Distributor's negligence in handling, storage, or transportation of Products.

ARTICLE 7 - INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

7.1 Trademark License. Manufacturer hereby grants Distributor a non-exclusive, non-transferable license to use the ClearSpring trademarks and trade dress solely in connection with the distribution and promotion of Products within the Territory.

7.2 Brand Standards. Distributor shall comply with Manufacturer's brand guidelines and shall submit all marketing materials using Manufacturer's trademarks for prior approval.

7.3 Ownership. All trademarks, trade names, and intellectual property associated with the Products shall remain the sole property of Manufacturer.

ARTICLE 8 - CONFIDENTIALITY

8.1 Each Party shall maintain the confidentiality of the other Party's confidential business information, including pricing, customer lists, sales data, and proprietary formulations.

8.2 Confidentiality obligations shall survive for three (3) years following termination of this Agreement.

ARTICLE 9 - TERM AND TERMINATION

9.1 Term. This Agreement shall have an initial term of three (3) years, automatically renewing for successive one (1) year terms unless either Party provides one hundred twenty (120) days' written notice of non-renewal.

9.2 Termination for Cause. Either Party may terminate upon thirty (30) days' written notice for material breach that remains uncured.

9.3 Termination for Failure to Meet Minimums. If Distributor fails to meet the Minimum Purchase Requirement for two (2) consecutive quarters, Manufacturer may, at its option: (a) convert Distributor's appointment from exclusive to non-exclusive; or (b) terminate this Agreement upon sixty (60) days' notice.

9.4 Post-Termination. Upon termination: (a) Distributor may sell remaining inventory for ninety (90) days; (b) Manufacturer shall repurchase unsold, unexpired inventory at Distributor's cost; and (c) all trademark licenses shall immediately terminate.

ARTICLE 10 - GENERAL PROVISIONS

10.1 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Florida.

10.2 Dispute Resolution. Disputes shall be submitted to binding arbitration in Tampa, Florida, under AAA Commercial Rules.

10.3 Force Majeure. Neither Party shall be liable for failures due to events beyond its reasonable control.

10.4 Independent Contractor. Distributor is an independent contractor and not an agent, employee, or partner of Manufacturer.

10.5 Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties.

10.6 Amendments. Amendments must be in writing and signed by both Parties.

10.7 Notices. All notices shall be in writing and sent to the addresses above.
