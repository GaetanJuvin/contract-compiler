CATERING SERVICES PROCUREMENT CONTRACT

Contract No.: CSC-2025-0478
Effective Date: October 15, 2025

This Catering Services Procurement Contract ("Contract") is entered into by and between:

CLIENT: Westfield Corporate Campus, LLC, a Maryland limited liability company, with its principal office at 9200 Corporate Boulevard, Columbia, MD 21046 ("Client")

CATERER: Refined Palate Catering Group, Inc., a District of Columbia corporation, with its principal office at 1828 L Street NW, Suite 600, Washington, DC 20036 ("Caterer")

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Client operates a multi-building corporate campus comprising six office buildings, two conference centers, and associated amenities serving approximately 4,500 employees and frequent visitors;

WHEREAS, Client desires to engage a professional catering company to provide food service operations for its corporate campus, including daily cafeteria service, executive dining, conference and event catering, and vending operations; and

WHEREAS, Caterer possesses the qualifications, experience, staff, and resources necessary to provide such services;

NOW, THEREFORE, the Parties agree as follows:

SECTION 1 - SCOPE OF SERVICES

1.1 Cafeteria Operations. Caterer shall operate the cafeteria facilities located in Buildings A, C, and E of Client's corporate campus ("Cafeteria Facilities"). Services shall include:
    (a) Breakfast service: 7:00 AM - 9:30 AM, Monday through Friday
    (b) Lunch service: 11:00 AM - 2:00 PM, Monday through Friday
    (c) Afternoon refresh: 2:00 PM - 4:00 PM, Monday through Friday
    (d) Menu offerings shall include at least four (4) entree options daily, including vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, and allergen-free selections

1.2 Executive Dining. Caterer shall operate the Executive Dining Room located on the 8th floor of Building A, serving breakfast and lunch to C-suite executives and their guests. Menu shall rotate weekly and shall feature premium ingredients and presentation.

1.3 Conference and Event Catering. Caterer shall provide catering services for conferences, meetings, corporate events, and special functions as requested by Client. Events may range from small executive meetings (8-12 persons) to large corporate events (up to 500 persons).

1.4 Pantry and Refreshment Service. Caterer shall stock and maintain pantries on each floor of each building with coffee, tea, filtered water, and light snacks as specified in Exhibit A.

1.5 Vending. Caterer shall operate and maintain vending machines throughout the campus as designated by Client, offering healthy snack options, beverages, and fresh food items.

SECTION 2 - STAFFING

2.1 Personnel. Caterer shall provide sufficient qualified personnel to perform all services under this Contract. Key positions shall include:
    (a) On-site General Manager (full-time, dedicated)
    (b) Executive Chef (full-time, dedicated)
    (c) Sous Chefs (minimum two, full-time, dedicated)
    (d) Line cooks, prep cooks, and service staff as needed
    (e) Event coordinator (full-time, dedicated)

2.2 Key Personnel. The individuals filling the positions of General Manager, Executive Chef, and Event Coordinator are considered "Key Personnel." Caterer shall not reassign Key Personnel without providing Client with at least thirty (30) days' prior written notice and introducing the proposed replacement for Client's approval, which shall not be unreasonably withheld.

2.3 Background Checks. All Caterer personnel working on Client's campus shall undergo background checks and drug screening in accordance with Client's security policies prior to commencing work on site.

2.4 Uniforms and Appearance. All Caterer personnel shall wear clean, professional uniforms bearing the Caterer's logo and individual name badges. Appearance standards shall comply with Client's workplace policies.

SECTION 3 - FOOD QUALITY AND SAFETY

3.1 Quality Standards. All food prepared and served by Caterer shall be of high quality, prepared from fresh ingredients, and presented in an appetizing manner. Caterer shall source ingredients locally where practicable and shall prioritize organic and sustainably produced items.

3.2 Food Safety. Caterer shall maintain a food safety management system in compliance with the FDA Food Safety Modernization Act (FSMA), local health department regulations, and ServSafe certification standards. All food handling personnel shall hold current ServSafe certifications.

3.3 Health Inspections. Caterer shall maintain a health inspection score of "A" or equivalent from the applicable local health department at all times. Caterer shall promptly notify Client of any health inspection results, citations, or corrective actions.

3.4 Allergen Management. Caterer shall implement a comprehensive allergen management program, including clear labeling of all menu items with the eight major allergens, staff training on allergen awareness, and protocols for handling allergen-related requests.

3.5 Nutritional Information. Caterer shall provide nutritional information for all menu items, including calorie counts, macronutrient data, and ingredient lists. Nutritional information shall be displayed at the point of service and available digitally.

SECTION 4 - FACILITIES AND EQUIPMENT

4.1 Client-Provided Facilities. Client shall provide the Cafeteria Facilities, Executive Dining Room, and pantry spaces in good working condition, including major kitchen equipment (ovens, refrigerators, dishwashers, ventilation systems) as inventoried in Exhibit B.

4.2 Maintenance. Caterer shall be responsible for the daily cleaning and routine maintenance of all food service areas and equipment. Client shall be responsible for major equipment repairs and capital replacements.

4.3 Smallwares. Caterer shall provide all smallwares, including cookware, utensils, serving dishes, table linens, and disposable items, at Caterer's expense.

4.4 Technology. Caterer shall implement a point-of-sale system compatible with Client's employee badge system for cashless transactions. Caterer shall also provide an online ordering system for conference catering and event bookings.

SECTION 5 - PRICING AND PAYMENT

[ANOMALY: Contradictory payment terms in 5.3 vs 5.4]
5.1 Management Fee. Client shall pay Caterer a monthly management fee of Forty-Five Thousand Dollars ($45,000) for the operation of Cafeteria Facilities and Executive Dining ("Management Fee").

5.2 Cafeteria Revenue. Cafeteria sales revenue from employee purchases shall be retained by Caterer. Caterer shall set menu prices at levels approved by Client, which shall be competitive with comparable corporate cafeteria operations in the greater Washington, DC metropolitan area.

5.3 Event Catering Pricing. Conference and event catering shall be billed at the per-person or per-event rates set forth in Exhibit C. Caterer shall invoice Client within five (5) business days following each event. Client shall pay event catering invoices within thirty (30) days of invoice receipt.

5.4 Payment Schedule. Client shall pay all invoices, including the Management Fee and event catering charges, within fifteen (15) business days of receipt. Late payments shall bear interest at the rate of one percent (1%) per month.

5.5 Annual Adjustments. The Management Fee shall be adjusted annually based on the Consumer Price Index for All Urban Consumers (CPI-U), Washington-Arlington-Alexandria metropolitan area, with a cap of four percent (4%) per annum.

5.6 Financial Reporting. Caterer shall provide Client with monthly financial reports detailing: (a) revenue by service category; (b) food costs and cost of goods sold; (c) labor costs; (d) other operating expenses; and (e) participation rates and transaction counts.

SECTION 6 - PERFORMANCE STANDARDS

6.1 Key Performance Indicators. Caterer's performance shall be measured against the following KPIs:
    (a) Employee satisfaction rating: minimum 4.0 out of 5.0 (measured quarterly)
    (b) Food quality score: minimum 90% (measured by Client's internal audit)
    (c) Health inspection score: "A" rating maintained at all times
    (d) Participation rate: minimum 55% of campus employees utilizing cafeteria services weekly
    (e) Event setup accuracy: 98% of events set up correctly and on time
    (f) Food waste reduction: year-over-year reduction of at least 5%

6.2 Performance Reviews. Client and Caterer shall conduct quarterly performance reviews. If Caterer fails to meet any KPI for two (2) consecutive quarters, Client may require a corrective action plan and, if not remediated within the following quarter, may terminate this Contract under Section 9.2.

6.3 Customer Feedback. Caterer shall implement a feedback system (digital kiosks and/or mobile app) to collect daily customer satisfaction data and shall respond to all complaints within twenty-four (24) hours.

SECTION 7 - INSURANCE

7.1 Caterer shall maintain throughout the Term:
    (a) Commercial General Liability: $2,000,000 per occurrence, $5,000,000 aggregate
    (b) Products/Completed Operations: $2,000,000
    (c) Liquor Liability (if applicable): $2,000,000
    (d) Workers' Compensation: as required by law
    (e) Commercial Auto: $1,000,000
    (f) Umbrella/Excess Liability: $5,000,000

7.2 Client shall be named as additional insured on CGL and Products policies.

SECTION 8 - INDEMNIFICATION

[ANOMALY: Missing indemnification from Caterer to Client for food safety incidents - one-sided provision]
8.1 Client Indemnification. Client shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Caterer from and against all claims, damages, losses, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from: (a) Client's negligence or willful misconduct; (b) defects in Client-provided facilities or equipment; or (c) Client's breach of this Contract.

8.2 Limitation of Liability. NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, SPECIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES ARISING UNDER THIS CONTRACT.

[Note: No reciprocal indemnification from Caterer to Client for foodborne illness, food safety incidents, or Caterer's breach is provided.]

SECTION 9 - TERM AND TERMINATION

9.1 Term. This Contract shall have an initial term of five (5) years from the Effective Date. Thereafter, it shall renew automatically for successive two (2) year terms unless either Party provides one hundred eighty (180) days' written notice of non-renewal.

9.2 Termination for Cause. Either Party may terminate this Contract upon sixty (60) days' written notice if the other Party materially breaches and fails to cure within such sixty (60) day period.

9.3 Termination for Convenience. Client may terminate this Contract for convenience upon one hundred twenty (120) days' written notice. In such event, Client shall pay Caterer: (a) all amounts due through the termination date; (b) unamortized capital investments made by Caterer specifically for the Client's operations, as documented and approved by Client; and (c) a termination fee equal to three (3) months of the Management Fee.

9.4 Transition. Upon termination or expiration, Caterer shall cooperate in an orderly transition to a successor caterer for a period of up to sixty (60) days, including providing access to recipes (excluding proprietary formulations), operating procedures, vendor relationships, and historical data.

SECTION 10 - SUSTAINABILITY

10.1 Caterer shall implement sustainable practices including: (a) composting and food waste diversion programs; (b) use of compostable or recyclable serviceware; (c) sourcing from local farms and sustainable fisheries; (d) elimination of single-use plastics; and (e) energy and water conservation measures.

10.2 Caterer shall provide Client with an annual sustainability report detailing initiatives, metrics, and progress toward goals.

SECTION 11 - GENERAL PROVISIONS

11.1 Governing Law. This Contract shall be governed by the laws of the State of Maryland.

11.2 Dispute Resolution. Disputes shall first be submitted to mediation. If unresolved within forty-five (45) days, either Party may pursue litigation in the courts of Howard County, Maryland.

11.3 Independent Contractor. Caterer is an independent contractor. Caterer's employees are not employees of Client for any purpose.

11.4 Permits and Licenses. Caterer shall obtain and maintain all permits, licenses, and certifications required for the operation of food service on Client's campus.

11.5 Force Majeure. Neither Party shall be liable for delays or failures due to events beyond its reasonable control.

11.6 Entire Agreement. This Contract and all Exhibits constitute the entire agreement.

11.7 Amendments. Amendments must be in writing and signed by both Parties.

11.8 Assignment. Neither Party may assign without the other's written consent.

11.9 Notices. All notices shall be in writing, delivered to the addresses above.
